# Create a Manuscript Record Form

Create a Manuscript Record Form (MRF) to begin tracking a publication in the Open Science Portal (OSP). The MRF stores manuscript details and supports the publication review process.

::: tip
**Save Your Progress**

Save your manuscript frequently to prevent losing work.

You can save your progress by:

- Clicking the circular **Floppy-Disk** button in the bottom-right corner of the page.
- Scrolling to the bottom of the form and clicking **Save**.

After saving, you can safely log out of the OSP.
:::

## Open the My Manuscripts Page

The **My Manuscripts** page lists all manuscript records you created or that have been shared with you.

### Steps

1. Hover over the left sidebar to expand the navigation menu.
2. Click **My Manuscripts**.

### Result

The **My Manuscripts** page appears and displays all manuscript records associated with your account, including drafts and shared manuscripts.

![Dashboard](/images/publication-process/my_manuscripts_page.png)

## Create a New Manuscript Record Form

Create a new MRF from the **My Manuscripts** page.

### Steps

1. Click **+ Create Manuscript** in the **Manuscripts** menu.
2. Select the **Desired Publication Type**.
3. Click **Continue**.
4. Enter the manuscript title in the **Title** field.
5. Select the **Lead Region** in the **DFO Regions** field.
6. Click **Continue**.
7. Click **Create**.

### Result

A new Manuscript Record Form is created and opened for editing.

### Additional Information

- Click **Back** to modify information entered in previous steps.
- Click **Cancel** to exit the manuscript creation process without saving.

## Navigate the Manuscript Record Form

The Manuscript Record Form is organized into sections that allow you to enter manuscript details.

![MRF Form Navigation](/images/publication-process/navigating_mrf.png)

### Additional Information

- **Section Menu** – Use the left sidebar to open sections such as **General Information**, **Authors**, **Peer Reviewers**, and **Funding Sources**.
- **Progress Indicators** – Sections with incomplete required fields are highlighted.
- **Required Fields** – Fields marked with an asterisk (*) must be completed before submitting the form.
- **Save Options** – Save your progress using the **Floppy-Disk** button or the **Save** button.
- **Form Status** – The top-right corner displays the manuscript status (for example, **Draft** or **In Review**).

## Understand the Section Menu

The **Section Menu** provides access to pages related to the Manuscript Record Form (MRF) and its review process.

### Additional Information

- **Form** – Opens the main MRF page where you complete and submit the manuscript record form.
- **Manuscript Management Review** – Opens the [Manuscript Management Review](./management-review-process.md) page and displays the current review status.
- **Manuscript Progress** – Opens the [Manuscript Progress](./manuscript-record-form.md#manuscript-progress) page and shows the workflow and current progress of the MRF.
- **Sharing** – Opens the [Sharing](./manuscript-record-form.md#sharing-a-mrf) page where you can share the MRF with additional users. Authors and management review users automatically have access and do not need to be added.
- **Publication** – Opens the associated [Publication](./publications.md) record if one exists. A publication record is created after the manuscript is marked as accepted for publication.

## Complete the Manuscript Record Form

After creating an MRF, the system assigns a unique ID and sets the status to **Draft**. You must complete all required fields before submitting the manuscript for management review.

![New MRF](/images/publication-process/new_mrf.png)

### Additional Information

- Fields marked with an asterisk (*) are required.
- Save your progress frequently to prevent data loss.

You can save your progress by:

- Clicking the circular **Floppy-Disk** button in the bottom-right corner of the page.
- Scrolling to the bottom of the form and clicking **Save**.

After saving, you can safely log out of the OSP.

## Upload a Manuscript

Upload a PDF version of your manuscript to the MRF. You can upload a newer version later if the manuscript changes.

### Steps

1. Click the **Drop the file or click to upload manuscript** field.
2. Select the manuscript PDF from the file browser.
3. Click **Open**.

### Result

The manuscript PDF is uploaded and attached to the Manuscript Record Form.

## Add Authors and Affiliations

Add all authors who contributed to the manuscript and specify their affiliations.

::: tip
**Default Affiliation**

**DFO** is selected as the default affiliation to speed up data entry. Change the affiliation when adding authors from other organizations.
:::

### Steps

Repeat these steps for each author:

1. Click **+**.
2. Search for the author in the **Author** field using their name or email.
3. Select **Yes** for **Corresponding Author** if the author will be the primary contact within DFO.
4. Select **Yes** for **Group Author** if the author represents a group.

If **Group Author** is selected:

- Search for the group by name.
- Select the group from the search results.

5. Click **Save**.

### Result

The selected author is added to the Manuscript Record Form. Added authors can view and edit the MRF but cannot delete it.

![Adding Authors](/images/publication-process/adding_authors.png)

## Change the DFO Corresponding Author

Set or change the DFO corresponding author for the manuscript.

::: important
The **DFO Corresponding Author** is the person responsible for answering questions about the manuscript within DFO. This may be different from the corresponding author listed in the published article.
:::

### Steps

1. Click the author's name.
2. Toggle the **DFO Corresponding Author** slider.

### Result

The author's **DFO Corresponding Author** status is updated.

### If an Author Does Not Appear in the Author List

If the author you are searching for does not appear in the list, create a new author record.

See [Create a New Author](/en/general/more-features#create-a-new-author).

### If a Group Author Does Not Appear in the Organization List

If the organization or group author does not appear in the list, create a new organization record.

See [Create a New Organization](/en/general/more-features#create-a-new-organization).

::: info
The OSP records **Group Authors** to identify organizations and working groups that contributed to the publication process. The **Author List** assigns credit for the manuscript.
:::

## Add a Plain Language Summary

Provide a plain language summary (PLS) to improve accessibility of the publication.

![Plain Language Summary](/images/publication-process/pls.png)

### Additional Information

- Plain Language Summaries are required for all scientific publications.
- The summary should be written for approximately an **8th-grade reading level** to support broader comprehension.
- Guidance on writing plain language content is available from the Government of Canada:  
  [Communications Community Office](https://www.canada.ca/en/privy-council/services/communications-community-office/communications-101-boot-camp-canadian-public-servants/plain-language-accessibility-inclusive-communications.html)

### Select the Source Language of the PLS

Choose the language used for the required plain language summary.

### Steps

1. Select **English** or **French** in the **Source Language of the PLS** field.

### Result

The selected language becomes the required language for the Plain Language Summary during management review.

### Additional Information

You may provide the summary in **both English and French**, but only the selected source language is required for submission.

### Generate a Plain Language Summary Draft

Generate a draft Plain Language Summary (PLS) using the manuscript abstract and the selected language.

::: note
The **Generate PLS** tool uses the Artificial Intelligence (AI) Large Language Model (LLM) [Llama 3.2](https://www.llama.com/), developed by Meta.

This tool runs entirely within the DFO network. Information submitted to the tool **does not leave the DFO intranet** and **cannot be transmitted to Meta or external systems**.

When using AI tools within the Government of Canada network, review the Treasury Board of Canada Secretariat guidance on responsible AI use:  
[Guide on the use of generative artificial intelligence](https://www.canada.ca/en/government/system/digital-government/digital-government-innovations/responsible-use-ai/guide-use-generative-ai.html)
:::

::: tip
The **Generate PLS** feature creates a draft based on your manuscript abstract. You can generate multiple drafts to refine the summary.

After finalizing the PLS, you can use **Translate PLS** to generate a version in the other official language.
:::

### Steps

1. Click **Generate PLS**.
2. Review the generated draft.
3. Edit the summary if needed.

### Result

A draft Plain Language Summary appears in the PLS text field.

### Additional Information

Save your changes after adding or editing the summary by:

- Clicking the **Floppy-Disk** icon in the bottom-right corner of the page.
- Scrolling to the bottom of the page and clicking **Save**.

## Set Report Licensing

Specify whether the report should be licensed under the **Open Government Licence – Canada**.

::: tip
This section is visible only for **DFO publications**.
:::

### Steps

1. Select **Yes** or **No** in the **Report Licensing** field.

### Conditional Steps

If **No** is selected:

- Enter an explanation describing why the **Open Government Licence – Canada** cannot be applied.

### Result

The selected licensing status is saved to the Manuscript Record Form.

### Additional Information

DFO follows the **Open by Design and by Default** principle of Open Science. Publications should be licensed under the latest version of the **Open Government Licence – Canada** whenever possible.

The licence should not be applied if conflicting licensing obligations exist.

## Indicate Open Publication

Specify whether the publication will be made openly accessible.

### Steps

1. Select **Yes** or **No** in the **Open Publication** field.

### Conditional Steps

If **No** is selected:

- Enter the rationale explaining why open access cannot be applied.

### Result

The manuscript record reflects whether the publication will be openly accessible.

### Additional Information

The **Open by Design and by Default** approach requires publications to be freely and publicly accessible unless an exception applies.

Exceptions may include situations where information, data, or code are restricted due to:

- research security
- proprietary interests
- third-party restrictions
- legal, ethical, or confidentiality agreements

## Submit the Manuscript for Management Review

Submit the Manuscript Record Form (MRF) once all required fields are complete.

::: important
The MRF **applicant or any listed author** can submit the manuscript for management review.

The user who submits the MRF automatically becomes the **MRF applicant**.

If the person who created the MRF is **not an author** (for example, an administrative assistant), they may lose access to the MRF after submission. An author can share the MRF with them again if needed.
:::

### Steps

1. Scroll to the bottom of the **Manuscript Record** page.
2. Click **Submit**.
3. Review the **Submission for Management Review** consent statement.
4. Click **Yes** to accept the consent.
5. Click **Next**.
6. Enter the name of the **Division Manager** in the search field.
7. Select the manager from the search results.
8. Click **Submit**.

### Conditional Steps

If the **Division Manager** does not appear in the search results:

1. Click **Can't find the user you're looking for?**.
2. Enter the manager's **First Name**, **Last Name**, **E-mail**, and **Preferred Language**.
3. Click **Invite**.

### Result

The manuscript is submitted for management review.

To confirm submission, check the **Status** box at the top-right of the **Manuscript Record** page. The status should display **In Review**.

## Submit the Manuscript for Publication

After completing the **Manuscript Management Review Process**, you can submit the manuscript through the **Single Window for Scientific Publications**.

### EOS Secondary Scientific and Technical Publication

::: warning
**Required Information**

You must obtain a **Catalogue Number** before submitting the manuscript to the Single Window.

To request a Catalogue Number:

1. Complete the **Request Form for Publication Numbers**.  
   [REQUEST FORM FOR PUBLICATION NUMBERS](https://intranet.ent.dfo-mpo.ca/mpo/sites/dfo-mpo/files/publishing-form-formulaire-publication-eng_0.pdf)
2. Email the completed form to [**Publications.XNCR@dfo-mpo.gc.ca**](mailto:Publications.XNCR@dfo-mpo.gc.ca).

## Submit the Manuscript to the Single Window

Submit the manuscript to the **Single Window for Scientific Publications** after completing the Manuscript Management Review Process.

![Submit to Single Window](/images/publication-process/submit_single_window.png)

### Steps

1. Open the Manuscript Record Form.
2. Select **Manuscript Progress** from the section menu.
3. Click **Submit Manuscript**.
4. Complete the form.

Complete the form:

- **Journal/Series** – Select the publication series.
- **Catalogue Number** – Enter the assigned catalogue number.
- **Copy of Manuscript** – Upload the manuscript file (.docx or .doc accepted).
- **ISBN** – Enter the ISBN if available.
- **Issue Number** – Enter the issue number if applicable.

5. Click **Submit**.

### Result

The manuscript is submitted to the **Single Window for Scientific Publications** for review and publication. You will receive a notification when the manuscript is published or if changes are required.

:::info
You may still upload your manuscript as a PDF, however, the Single Window will not be able to make edits if required. If edits are required the Single Window team will contact you via email.
:::

## Submit to a Third-Party Journal

Submit the manuscript to a journal after completing the Manuscript Management Review Process.

Continue updating the Manuscript Record Form throughout the publication process to improve publishing time metrics and increase visibility of the work.

### Record the Initial Submission to a Journal

### Steps

1. Open the Manuscript Record Form.
2. Select **Manuscript Progress** from the section menu.
3. Click **Mark as Submitted** under **Initial Submission for Publication**.
4. Enter the submission date in the **Submitted to Journal On** field.

You can:

- Type the date in **YYYY-MM-DD** format, or
- Select the date using the **Calendar** icon.

5. Click **Update**.

### Result

The Manuscript Record Form records the date the manuscript was submitted to the journal.
## Record Manuscript Acceptance

Record when the manuscript has been accepted for publication.

![Accept for Publication](/images/publication-process/accept_for_publication.png)

### Steps

1. Open the Manuscript Record Form.
2. Select **Manuscript Progress** from the section menu.
3. Click **Mark as Accepted**.
4. Complete the form.

Complete the form:

- **Submitted to Journal On** – Update the submission date if required.
- **Accepted for Publication On** – Enter the acceptance date.
- **Journal/Series** – Select the journal or publication series.

5. Click **Update**.

### Conditional Steps

If the journal is not listed:

- Email the **OSP Support Team** to request that the journal be added:  
  DFO.OpenScience-ScienceOuverte.MPO@dfo-mpo.gc.ca

### Result

The Manuscript Record Form is updated to show that the manuscript has been accepted for publication.

## Share a Manuscript Record Form

Share access to a Manuscript Record Form (MRF) with another user, such as an administrator or section head.

::: tip
DFO authors and reviewers associated with the MRF already have access based on their role and do not need to be shared manually.
:::

![Sharing](/images/publication-process/sharing.png)

### Steps

1. Open the manuscript from the **My Manuscript Records** page.
2. Select **Sharing** from the section menu.
3. Click **Share**.
4. Enter the user's name or email in the **User** search field.
5. Select the user from the search results.
6. Select the access level.

Choose one of the following:

- **Can Edit** – Allows the user to view and edit the MRF.
- **Can Delete** – Allows the user to delete the MRF.

7. (Optional) Set an expiration date in the **Expires On** field.
8. Click **Share**.

### Conditional Steps

If the user does not appear in the search results:

- Follow the steps in **[Invite a User](/en/general/more-features#invite-a-user)**.

### Result

The selected user receives access to the Manuscript Record Form with the assigned permissions.

![New Share](/images/publication-process/new_share.png)
